This Is Me!

Who am I?

I’m Emily Stevens and I’m a junior at the Early College at Guilford. I’m a competitive swimmerfor Star Aquatics and a team captain for the Western Guilford High School varsity swim team. The ongoing pandemic has shown me that more than anything, I need to be flexible in planning and to seize opportunities. When the pandemic hit, I was swimming every afternoon with a large group of teammates, so that had to stop abruptly. I was volunteering at the Greensboro Science Center, a service activity that I have been unable to participate in for an entire year. I was going to school, preparing for advanced placement exams. Suddenly, to protect the health and safety of myself and others, I was not allowed to leave my house for multiple months. This pandemic forced me to make decisions for myself and others that could potentially prevent lives of individuals—strangers—from being at risk. I have become stronger both physically and mentally because of this pandemic. During this crucial stage of development in my life, I have managed to become a better person.

Moving On

When the pandemic hit, I could have chosen to disobey the orders of Governor Cooper and continued hanging out with friends and not abiding by his guidelines, but I chose to stay home and do my part to help others. I also decided to begin working harder at building more physical strength while I was being pushed to the limits mentally. I began training more aggressively for the sport of ninja. With the help of my mom, I built a ninja course in my backyard and was able to become stronger without a gym or fitness facility. The virus helped to begin my ninja career and make me the strongest I have ever been and changed my mental perspective about the lockdown. I could have thought about the pandemic as a time that ruined my year and caused me to miss out on crucial parts of my life, but I chose a different route. Instead, the pandemic was a time for me to get back on the path I was meant to take and become a better person, in life and in sports. The pandemic gave me the time I needed to “get back on my feet” after a tough year and begin bounding toward my future.
